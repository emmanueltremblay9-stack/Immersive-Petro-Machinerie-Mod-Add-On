Industrial Driller Garage
Tunnel Digger service garage
<&platform>The Industrial Driller Garage is an 11x13x10 validated service structure for the Immersive Machinery Tunnel Digger, including its lower hempcrete foundation layer. The visible above-grade service garage remains 11x13x9. It is controlled by the Docking Controller on the rear service wall and extends forward into a ramped open service bay.<np>
The garage is not a temporary block or a single controller shell. The required hempcrete foundation, frame, reinforced rear service wall, two rear 3x3x3 tanks, ramp, rails, service arms, and hollow bay clearance must be present before IPM services are considered available.<np>
<&controller>Place the Docking Controller at the rear center of the garage. Local coordinates use the controller as (0,0,0), with x=-5..5, y=-2..7, and z=-12..0. The front is negative Z, where the Tunnel Digger approaches. The Survey Console sits directly above the controller at (0,1,0).<np>
After building the garage, use the Docking Controller to validate the real-block structure and open the maintenance GUI. The Immersive Engineering Engineer's Hammer path is validation-only in this phase. The experimental formed garage shell is disabled because fuel tanks, GUI, survey, soft lock, and Tunnel Digger detection still belong to the real-block controller fallback.<np>
Immersive Petroleum Projector support uses the same 11x13x10 structure as the manual preview. In creative mode, the Projector can place the required real blocks, but it does not replace the controller validation step and it does not clear unrelated blocks left inside the hollow bay. If a projection is already locked in the world, sneak-use clears the projection instead of direct-building it. For direct build testing, select the Industrial Driller Garage, keep the projection unplaced, aim at the intended footprint, then sneak-use in creative mode. If validation still fails, use the Docking Controller chat message; it reports the exact local coordinate, world coordinate, expected block, and found block.<np>
<&structural_blocks>The garage uses IE-style steel scaffolding, engineering blocks, sheetmetal, fluid pipes, pumps, and steel fence for most of its frame. IPM blocks are used for IPM-specific functions: Docking Controller, Fuel Port, Lubricant Port, Output Port, Repair Bay, Upgrade Bay, Survey Console, and Locking Rails.<np>
Construction overview:<br>
Lower foundation y=-2: IE hempcrete fills the lower footing below the garage footprint, including under the heavy engineering anchor area.<br>
Foundation y=-1: straight steel-scaffolding bases under the side support pillars, the lower steel-scaffolding approach ramp at z=-12..-11, and hempcrete filled gaps around the base.<br>
Raised service floor y=0: the ramp rises into the Locking Rail guide lane from z=-10 to z=-1, with steel scaffolding service deck edges, side walkways, and hempcrete gap fill where it does not block the ramp approach or functional blocks.<br>
Rear service area: two real 3x3x3 steel-sheetmetal tanks sit behind a stronger service wall. Fuel is rear-left at x=-4..-2, y=1..3, z=-2..0. Lubricant is rear-right at x=2..4, y=1..3, z=-2..0. Each tank is one block inward from the side border so the outer steel-scaffolding pillars stay straight from y=-1 through the top armature. Heavy engineering footings remain at the inner tank bases, an open center service window above the controller, and a pipe header at y=4 make the back read as a service wall instead of a flat scaffold face.<br>
Lower/mid levels y=1..5: the central bay remains hollow air for the Tunnel Digger, except high fluid-arm endpoints near z=-7.<br>
Upper levels y=6..7: steel fence crane beams, inner side service rails, and scaffolding crossbeams form the overhead service armature.<np>

This clearance is what lets the Tunnel Digger enter the service garage instead of colliding with a solid block cube.<np>

<&functional_blocks>The Fuel Port stores approved dock fuel, currently diesel and gasoline. The Lubricant Port stores lubricant. These tanks belong to the garage, not to the Tunnel Digger. The Output Port, Repair Bay, and Upgrade Bay are reserved for later maintenance systems. The Survey Console is reserved for the Core Sampling Scan flow, but final IE/IP survey output is contained while the Garage-installed upgrade scope is reviewed.<np>
Current stable workflow: build the full real-block garage with manual placement or the IP Projector, use the Docking Controller to validate it, bring the Tunnel Digger up the ramp into the hollow center lane on the raised service floor, use the controller to confirm detection and alignment, sneak-use the controller to soft-lock or unlock, and open the maintenance screen. In this phase, Survey Console use returns a containment message instead of generating survey output.<np>
The Core Sampling Upgrade concept turns the Tunnel Digger into a mobile sampling station through the IPM garage upgrade path. It is intended to output native Immersive Engineering core samples and native Immersive Petroleum survey results for the current chunk only after the scan flow is finalized. It is not long-range radar and does not scan multiple chunks.<np>
Future bore head progression is planned around industrial sizes only: 5x5 as the base heavy steel head, 7x7 as reinforced steel, and 9x9 as late-game diamond-tipped or netherite-tipped steel. Mining size changes are not part of this manual entry and remain a later high-risk phase.
